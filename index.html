Praxis

Graph Neural Network (GNN)-powered eDiscovery that learns alongside your reviewers

The eDiscovery Bottleneck

In any legal investigation, teams must sift through tens of thousands of documents to find the ones that matter. The industry standard — keyword search — is fundamentally broken: it returns every document that mentions your search terms, regardless of relevance, and misses critical documents that use different phrasing. The result? Legal teams spend weeks reviewing documents at $300+/hour, with no way to know what they're overlooking or when they're done.

Our Approach

Praxis replaces keyword search with a Graph Neural Network that learns from reviewer feedback. You label a small seed set of documents. The GNN studies the relationship graph — who talks to whom, which topics cluster together — and ranks the entire corpus by relevance. You review the top batch, provide more labels, and the model retrains. Each cycle makes the model smarter and the reviewer faster. Training stops when recall plateaus — typically after reviewing less than 20% of the corpus.

Let's ground this in a real case study: the Enron investigation.
